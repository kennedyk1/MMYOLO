ATTENTION RECOMMENDATIONS

MATRIX

SCENARIO: raw 6 channels

- option: no attention
  recommendation: acceptable
  reason: clean baseline

- option: early ChannelAttention
  recommendation: recommended
  reason: helps weight raw modalities and channels

- option: early SpatialAttention
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: there is usually too little semantic structure at the beginning

- option: CBAM too early
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: the spatial component is likely to be premature

- option: CBAM after the first C3k2
  recommendation: acceptable
  reason: features are already less raw at that point

- option: manual ChannelAttention + SpatialAttention at the same point
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: this becomes a hand-built CBAM with unnecessary complexity

- option: CBAM + ChannelAttention at the same point
  recommendation: redundant
  reason: CBAM already includes channel attention

- option: CBAM + SpatialAttention at the same point
  recommendation: redundant
  reason: CBAM already includes spatial attention

- option: CBAM + ChannelAttention + SpatialAttention
  recommendation: redundant
  reason: this repeats the same idea three times

- option: several attention modules stacked in the stem
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: too much complexity too early


SCENARIO: multi-stem

- option: no attention
  recommendation: acceptable
  reason: strong and well-structured baseline

- option: ChannelAttention after fusion
  recommendation: recommended
  reason: a very good point to weight already processed modalities

- option: CBAM after fusion
  recommendation: recommended
  reason: this is the best place to use CBAM

- option: SpatialAttention after fusion
  recommendation: acceptable
  reason: there is already more useful spatial structure

- option: ChannelAttention in every stem in the first test
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: too many variables at once

- option: CBAM in every stem
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: heavy and unnecessary for an initial experiment

- option: ChannelAttention in every stem + another one after fusion
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: probably too much for a first ablation

- option: CBAM after fusion + ChannelAttention at the same point
  recommendation: redundant
  reason: CBAM already covers that function

- option: CBAM after fusion + SpatialAttention at the same point
  recommendation: redundant
  reason: same reason

- option: several CBAM blocks spread across the early network
  recommendation: avoid
  reason: high cost and poor experimental readability


INTERACTION WITH YOLO26

- point: near C2PSA
  recommendation: avoid changing this in the first experiment

- point: on top of the final C3k2(..., attn=True) block
  recommendation: avoid this at the beginning

- point: before the backbone shares multimodal information
  recommendation: good place for ChannelAttention in raw 6-channel setups

- point: immediately after stem fusion
  recommendation: best place for CBAM in multi-stem setups


TOP 3 OPTIONS

1. raw 6 channels + early ChannelAttention
2. multi-stem without attention
3. multi-stem + post-fusion CBAM
